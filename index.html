Sacred Shrines of Kashi

Explore the prominent temples and historical sanctuaries of Varanasi, holding thousands of years of worship, wisdom, and mythological significance. Click on tabs inside cards to check timings and facts!

Jyotirlinga

Kashi Vishwanath Mandir

Known as the Golden Temple, it is dedicated to Lord Shiva (Vishweshwara), the supreme patron deity of Banaras. It houses one of the twelve sacred Jyotirlingas. Rebuilt in 1780 by Queen Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore, its majestic domes are plated with over 800 kilograms of pure gold. The temple stands as a beacon of ultimate liberation (Moksha) and divine light.

  • Mangala Aarti: 3:00 AM - 4:00 AM
  • Bhog Aarti: 11:15 AM - 12:20 PM
  • Sapta Rishi Aarti: 7:00 PM - 8:15 PM
  • Shayan Aarti: 10:30 PM - 11:00 PM
  • General Darshan: 6:00 AM - 9:00 PM
Architectural Style Nagara (Classic North Indian)
Spiritual Sign One of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva
Historical Builder Rani Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore (1780)
Gold Donation Maharaja Ranjit Singh of Punjab (1000 kg, 1835)
Importance: Supreme Spiritual Seat Deity: Lord Shiva
Buddhist Sanctuary

Sarnath Stupa

Located 10 km north of Varanasi, Sarnath is one of the four holiest Buddhist pilgrimage sites. Here, under the shade of the forest, Gautama Buddha delivered his first sermon (Dharmachakrapravartana) to his disciples. The monumental Dhamek Stupa stands 43.6 meters tall as a solid cylindrical tower of stone and brick, representing the birth of the Buddhist Sangha.

  • Opening Hours: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM (Daily)
  • Museum Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Closed Fridays)
  • Best Visit Time: October to March (Sunset view)
  • Entry Ticket: ₹25 (Indians), ₹300 (Foreigners)
Historical Origin Built by Emperor Ashoka (approx. 249 BCE)
Key Monument Dhamek Stupa & Ashoka Lion Capital (National Emblem)
Event First Sermon (Turning the Wheel of Dharma)
Materials Used Carved red sandstone and structural bricks
Importance: First Sermon of Buddha Type: Buddhist Heritage
Devotional Shrine

Sankat Mochan Mandir

Founded by the poet-saint Goswami Tulsidas in the early 16th century, this historic temple is dedicated to Lord Hanuman. The name 'Sankat Mochan' translates to the 'Reliever of Sorrows and Obstacles'. Situated along the Assi River bed, the temple is filled with a peaceful, forested vibe and a large family of monkeys, serving as a peaceful sanctuary of faith.

  • Morning Shift: 5:00 AM - 12:00 PM
  • Afternoon Pause: Closed 12:00 PM - 3:00 PM
  • Evening Shift: 3:00 PM - 12:00 AM
  • Special Days: Tuesdays & Saturdays (Huge Crowds)
Founder Sant Goswami Tulsidas (1500s)
Special Sweet Besan Laddoo (Prasad offered to deity)
Unique Statue Hanuman facing his lord, Lord Rama
Music Festival Sankat Mochan Sangeet Samaroh (Annual)
Importance: Removes Obstacles
Literary Heritage

Tulsi Manas Mandir

Built in 1964 out of pristine white marble, this temple holds immense cultural importance as it stands on the exact spot where Tulsidas composed the Ramcharitmanas—the Hindi translation of the Ramayana. The entire epic is beautifully engraved in fine scripts on the marble walls of the temple temple hall, accompanied by colorful mechanical models of Hindu mythology.

  • Daily Timing: 5:30 AM - 12:00 PM
  • Afternoon Break: Closed 12:00 PM - 3:30 PM
  • Evening Hours: 3:30 PM - 8:30 PM
  • Light Show: 6:00 PM onwards (Mythological models)
Construction Year 1964 (By Thakur Family of Benares)
Materials White Marble walls and columns
Artwork Entire Ramcharitmanas verses engraved
Exhibition Puppet show of Rama-Charitra (First Floor)
Importance: Origin of Ramcharitmanas
Mythological Sangam

Markandey Mahadev

Located 30 km from Varanasi at Kaithi, this highly sacred temple sits at the serene confluence of the Ganges and Gomati rivers. According to the Puranas, this is where the teenage sage Markandeya clung to the Shiva Linga to save his life. Lord Shiva appeared to defeat Yamaraj (Death), granting Markandeya immortality. It is highly visited for health and protection.

  • General Entry: 4:00 AM - 10:00 PM
  • Special Mondays: 3:00 AM - 11:30 PM
  • Shravan month: Open 24 hours during festivals
  • Pradosh Vrat: Special Evening Sandhya Aarti (6:30 PM)
Holy Location Kaithi village, Confluence of Ganga & Gomati
Reference Markandeya Purana Chapters
Key Boon Mahamrityunjaya protection from untimely death
Status Considered spiritual equivalent of Jyotirlinga
Confluence: Ganga & Gomati

Spirituality in Motion

Banaras is not just a geographical place; it is a spiritual frequency. From the quiet morning hours filled with holy temple chants and bells to the glowing lights of the evening worship, the city pulses with cultural richness.

"Older than history, older than tradition, older even than legend, and looks twice as old as all of them put together."

Subah-e-Banaras

The morning begins before sunrise at Assi Ghat. The scent of incense spreads as priests perform the morning Aarti, followed by Vedic chants, yoga, and classical morning ragas played on the sitar and shehnai, greeting the sun as it rises over the Ganges.

Daily Ganga Aarti

As night falls, Dashashwamedh Ghat lights up for the daily Ganga Aarti. Young priests dress in saffron robes and move large brass lamps weighing over 15 kilograms in rhythmic patterns, offering prayers to Mother Ganges. The sound of bells, conch shells, and chanting echoes as thousands of lit clay lamps float on the river under the starlight.
